Frequently Asked

Legal Questions Answered

What is included in the initial free consultation?

The initial consultation is a confidential 60-minute session conducted in person, by video, or telephone. Advocate Mehta reviews the core facts, assesses legal options, explains the likely process and timelines, and answers all preliminary questions. There is no obligation to retain services following the consultation.

Can Need Help represent clients located outside India?

Yes. We regularly represent clients in the United Kingdom, United Arab Emirates, Singapore, Canada and Australia. For matters requiring local court appearance in foreign jurisdictions, we work with vetted correspondent counsel and maintain active desks in Dubai and Singapore.

How is your fee structure determined?

Fees are structured transparently based on complexity, jurisdictions involved and anticipated time commitment. We offer fixed-fee arrangements for defined work, hourly billing for advisory matters, and contingency structures for eligible litigation cases. All arrangements are documented in a formal engagement letter before work commences.

How does Need Help maintain client confidentiality?

All communications are protected by attorney-client privilege under the Advocates Act 1961 and applicable international standards. We maintain encrypted document management systems, enforce strict non-disclosure protocols and never share client information without express written consent.

What is the typical timeline for resolving a commercial dispute?

Simple contract disputes may settle within 3–6 months. Complex High Court litigation may take 18–36 months. International arbitration typically concludes within 12–24 months. We provide a realistic timeline assessment during the initial consultation.

Does Need Help handle emergency or urgent legal matters?

Yes. We maintain a 24/7 emergency contact line for wrongful arrests, freezing order applications, emergency injunctions and immigration detention. Urgent matters are assessed immediately and filed before the appropriate court within 24–48 hours.
